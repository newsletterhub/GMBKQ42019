Is the boost in contract activity a positive sign for the Brooklyn sales market in 2020?
I'm pleased to announce the release of the Compass 4Q2019 Brooklyn Market Report for your review.
 
There were 2,695 recorded or closed sales in the 4Q2019, a slight decrease (2%) from a year ago and price discounts on average increased to 8% (vs. 6.5% in the prior quarter). However, other performance metrics demonstrate a positive momentum and signs of a stabilizing market:
 
  • The median sale price increased 6% to $800,000 from $752,000 last year, while the average sale price increased 0.8% to $959,103 from $951,384 in 2018.
 
  • The upward trend in average price was the highest of any fourth quarter and median price was the highest on record, reflecting in part the increase in new development condo sales as well as the general expansion taking place in the borough.
     
  • In the 4Q, apartments priced $2M and under comprised 94% of all unit sales. This market segment remains active and buyers seem more optimistic, reflecting in part, record low mortgage rates as well as a healthy NYC economy and growing tech sector.
  • There were 1,866 signed contracts across Brooklyn in the 4Q, a 12% increase from the same time last year.
     
  • Average days on market decreased to 137, lower than both last year and the prior quarter (148).
     
  • Active listings declined 9.5% to 4,516 units (vs. 4,992 in 4Q2018) and months of supply decreased to 7.4 (vs. 8.5 in 4Q2018).
 
  • North Brooklyn was the best performing submarket with the highest increase in average price (9%) and PPSF (4%), respectively (average price at $1,02,788 and PPSF at $1,041).
     
  • Although Northwest Brooklyn represents the most expensive submarket (average price at $1,403,362 and PPSF at $1,069), it experienced the greatest average price decline at 9%.
     
  • Properties priced $2M and higher comprised just 6% of all recorded sales, but offered the largest price discounts (11%+) and PPSF declined on average by 5%.
     
  • The $3M+ segment saw much larger properties (3,555-avg. sf) enter contract this quarter (9%) and were 6% more affordable compared to this period last year.
     
  • Northwest Brooklyn accounted for 33% of all signed contracts; these properties were the quickest to move, with 41% entering contract in the first 59 days, but there was little change in average price or PPSF from a year ago.

Note: Months of supply represents the number of months it would take to absorb all active inventory based on the trailing 12 months average of contract activity. Typically 6 to 9 months of supply indicates market equilibrium. Please note this analysis does not include ‘shadow inventory’ of unsold, unlisted new development inventory.
In a market with abundant inventory (including new development condos) and a climate of uncertainty, buyers are exercising caution, but also seizing purchase opportunities where they perceive value. As more sellers price strategically or are willing to discount to meet buyers demands on price, expectations are for further increased contract activity leading to future market stabilization.

Note: The Compass report addresses resale activity only and does not include new development condominiums or shadow inventory.
